### Segment-Level Performance
- The company is most profitable in the **Government, Small Business, and Channel Partners** segments.
- The **Enterprise segment is loss-making**, with negative profitability, indicating structural pricing or servicing issues.
- Profit margins by segment:
  - Channel Partners: **73%**
  - Midmarket: **28%**
  - Government: **22%**
  - Small Business: **10%**
  - Enterprise: **-3%**

- While Government and Small Business generate a large share of revenue, **profitability is concentrated in Channel Partners and Midmarket**.
- Enterprise customers consistently operate at a loss, suggesting a misalignment between pricing, discounts, and cost-to-serve.

---

### Geographic Performance
- Most profitable countries:
  - Germany
  - France
  - Canada
- Least profitable markets:
  - USA
  - Mexico

- Profit margins by country:
  - Germany: **15.66%**
  - France: **15.53%**
  - Canada: **14.18%**
  - Mexico: **13.88%**
  - USA: **11.97%**

- While the **USA leads in total sales**, Germany and France outperform in profitability, indicating stronger pricing power and cost efficiency in European markets.

---

### Product-Level Insights
- **Paseo and VTT** drive the majority of revenue and profit through scale.
- **Amarilla** delivers the highest margin, indicating strong pricing efficiency.
- **Velo** shows weaker profitability relative to sales, suggesting pricing or cost inefficiencies.
- Lower-tier products contribute minimally to profit and should be reviewed for optimization or rationalization.

---

### Time & Growth Trends
- Sales and profitability show strong **seasonality**, peaking in **Q4**, with **October** as the highest-performing month.
- Early-year months experience consistent slowdowns, while mid-year performance remains stable.
- Rapid revenue growth from **2013 to 2014** was achieved while maintaining stable margins, indicating scalable operations and effective cost control during expansion.

---

### Structural Profitability Drivers
- Profitability is driven more by **segment economics than geography**.
- Channel Partners and Government segments generate consistent profits across all regions.
- Enterprise remains loss-making in every market, confirming a structural pricing and cost issue.

### 2. Reassess Enterprise Strategy Urgently

- Apply segment-specific discount strategies to protect margins.
- Monitor discount impact on profitability, not just volume.

- Optimize or reposition low-margin products.
- Strengthen pricing and cost control for underperforming offerings like Velo.
- Double down on high-margin products such as Amarilla.
